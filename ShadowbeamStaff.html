The Shadowbeam Staff is a Magic weapon that is rarely dropped by Necromancers in the Dungeon after entering hardmode. It can be easily one of the strongest weapon in-game if you make an area designed to let it bounce off. It is effectively identical to the Necromancer's standard attack, creating a purple 'beam' that bounces off terrain. It can strike each enemy in its path only once, but it can otherwise strike an unlimited number of enemies within the beam's relatively short lifespan.

Through careful aim, the beam can be used to strike enemies that the user does not have a direct line of sight to. This makes the Shadowbeam Staff very useful in close quarters, such as in the Dungeon itself.

Notes

  • It has a 3.33% chance that it will drop from a Necromancer.
  • The beam will travel approximately 108 blocks before dissipating.
  • The best Modifier for the Shadowbeam Staff is Mythical.
  • Beams will damage each enemy only once per cast, but can damage any number of enemies.
    Note: Beams do less damage per consecutive enemy hit.
  • Useful for quick exploration - the beam emits a large amount of light and tends bounce around corners and illuminate entire rooms.
  • Very effective in the dungeon.
  • Due to its high DPS, low mana cost, and massive range, the Shadowbeam Staff is a very good magic weapon.
  • This weapon is particularly effective against long, segmented enemies like Wyverns if fired at their whole length. However, 'diminishing returns' when used against them post 1.2.0.3 has rendered it far less effective against enemies such as the Destroyer, as only 5 or so of the many hits will hit for any significant damage.
  • Prior to 1.2.0.3, it had the one of the highest DPS of any weapon, if it was able to hit several enemies at the same time.

Bugs

Bug : When the Shadowbeam Staff is fired at an angled block, the projected beam can 'wrap around' the block and pass through other blocks or doors directly above.
Bug : Occasionally the Shadow Beam will penetrate through walls 1-block thick, as if rebounding through an invisible hole.

History

  • 1.2.0.3: Beams now have diminishing returns.
  • 1.2: Introduced.
