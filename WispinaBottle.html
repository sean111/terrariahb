This information (or parts of it) applies only to the PC version of Terraria.

The Wisp in a Bottle summons a wisp, a floating ghostly skull, to provide light. It serves the same purpose as the Shadow Orb or the Fairy Bell, but the Wisp not only keeps pace with the player, it actually moves ahead in the direction the player is moving. This can be used to see a short distance through walls. It cannot be used together with the Shadow Orb or the Fairy Bell, and summoning either will dismiss the other. The Wisp in a Bottle can rarely be dropped from Blue Armored Bones, Hell Armored Bones, and Rusty Armored Bones.

Notes

  • You can direct the Wisp to move vertically by pressing the up (W) and down (S) keys. If you grapple onto a block, you can also direct the Wisp left (A) and right (D). This means that when you are using the Hoverboard, it will remain underneath you no matter your preference. Unlike other summonable light sources, when summoned, the Wisp in a Bottle initiates the same sound effect as eating a mushroom or a goldfish.
  • Since the Wisp will always try to stay above (or in the case of pressing a movement key, ahead of) you, the light will always be in the wall when mining horizontal shafts & when digging upward. In this case, the Fairy Bell is more effective for lighting when digging. On the other hand, the Wisp's movement capability can be useful for finding natural openings when mining underground.
  • The Wisp has two movement states. The first state keeps smooth pace with the player, yet sometimes falls a bit behind. This occurs when the player and the Wisp are moving at a relatively slow speed. In the second state, the skull begins a fast-paced and self-centered rotation, and moves at the same speed of the player; leaving behind a trail of particles and keeping pace with them at any given speed. The only exception is that, when falling, it can take a moment for the Wisp to reach the player's position, but it will soon catch up and continue pace. The second state begins when the player is moving at a relatively high speed, wherein the first state the Wisp could not keep pace with the player.
  • When grappled on a surface the player can control the Wisp using the movement keys.
Bug : When underwater, the Wisp seems to move abnormally when entering high-speed mode, spinning in small circles and slightly wobbling side to side. Apparently this is caused by the slowing effect of the water, which doesn't interact well with the Wisp movement pattern and speed.
Bug : If there is an enemy that has less than full health, and the Wisp's body is on top of it, the color of the enemy's health bar will change.
Anomaly: Using the Wisp in a Bottle triggers the sound used when eating a Mushroom or Goldfish.

Trivia

  • The Wisp in a Bottle might be a reference to the series The Legend of Zelda, particularly Ocarina of Time when Link is adult, where you can capture ghosts with empty bottles and sell them or use them later.
  • Wisp may refer to Will-O'-the-Wisps, mythological creatures that lead travelers to their death in swamps.
