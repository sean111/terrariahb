Merchant

Merchant
Statistics
Type Town
Max Life 250
Defense 15
This is a town NPC. You may need a home to get him/her

The Merchant is an NPC vendor that will spawn once all players in the game world have more than 50 between them in their inventories.

Items sold

Once the Merchant has moved into a house, he sells the following:

Inventory

Item Cost Availability
Mining Helmet 8 Always available.
Piggy Bank 1 Always available.
Iron Anvil 50 Always available.
Copper Pickaxe 5 Always available.
Copper Axe 4 Always available.
Torch 50 Always available.
Lesser Healing Potion 3 Always available.
Lesser Mana Potion 1 After the player has reached maximum base mana (200)
Wooden Arrow 10 Always available.
Shuriken 20 Always available.
Glowstick 10 During the night.
Rope 10 Always available.
Marshmallow 1 When the merchant's house is in a snow biome. (Natural or artificial)
Flare 7 When the player possesses a Flare Gun.
Blue Flare 7 When the player possesses a Flare Gun.
Throwing Knife 50 During a Blood Moon.
Safe 20 If Skeletron is Defeated.
Disco Ball 1 If Wall of Flesh is defeated.
Gold Dust 17 If Wall of Flesh is defeated.
Sickle 1 During Halloween.


Note: As with all NPCs, right-clicking an item will allow multiple purchases rather than purchasing it one by one.

Quotes

Morning:

  • "Sword beats paper! Get one today."
  • "Lovely morning, wouldn't you say? Was there something you needed?"
  • "You want apples? You want carrots? You want pineapples? We got torches."

Afternoon:

  • "The sun is high, but my prices are not."
  • "Boy, that sun is hot! I do have some perfectly ventilated armor."
  • "Check out my dirt blocks, they are extra dirty."

Evening:

  • "Ah, they will tell tales of [Name of Player] some day... good ones I'm sure."
  • "Night be upon us soon, friend. Make your choices while you can."
  • "You have no idea how much Dirt Blocks sell for overseas."

Beginning of night:

  • "Kosh, kapleck Mog. Oh sorry, that's klingon for 'Buy something or die.'"
  • "[Name of Player] is it? I've heard good things, friend!"

Midnight:

  • "I wonder if the moon is made of cheese... huh, what? Oh yes, buy something!"
  • "The last guy who was here left some junk... er I mean... treasures!"
  • "Did you say gold? I'll take that off of ya'."

End of night:

  • "I hear there's a secret treasure... oh never mind." (This quote may refer the Gold Chest found on Floating Islands)
  • "Angel statue you say? I'm sorry, I'm not a junk dealer." (This quote, however, is a lie as he apparently tries to sell Angel Statues to the Dryad.)

Blood Moon:

  • "Have you seen Chith...Shith.. Chat... The big eye?"
  • "Keep your eye on the prize, buy a lens!" (He does not actually sell lenses though)
  • "Oh, great. I can hear [Name of Mechanic] and [Name of Nurse] arguing from here." (Only if Nurse and Mechanic are present.)
  • "Not even a Blood Moon can stop capitalism. Let's do some business."
  • "Hey, this house is secure, right? Right? [Name of Player]?"

When Eye of Cthulhu has NOT been defeated:

  • "I hope a scrawny kid like you isn't all that's standing between us and Cthulu's Eye."
  • "I feel like an evil presence is watching me." (when the natural requirements for Eye of Cthulhu to spawn is acquired)
  • "Look at that shoddy armor you're wearing. Better buy some more healing potions."
Anomaly: In the Merchant's quote, the word "Cthulu" is actually written, rather than "Cthulhu".

Notes

  • The Merchant being unable to pronounce "Cthulhu" during a Blood Moon is reference to the fact that H.P. Lovecraft, Cthulhu's creator, intended for the name to be impossible to pronounce.
  • At the beginning of the night, the Merchant may say "Kosh, kapleck Mog. Oh sorry, that's Klingon for 'Buy something or die.'", when "Buy something or die" is actually translated as "vay' blje'be'chugh vaj blHegh" in Klingon
  • The merchant makes several quotes referencing things he does not sell: armor and swords in addition despite claiming they sell for alot overseas, the merchant gives you no money for dirt blocks
Bug : In 1.2.0.3.1 when an NPC dies they may be replaced by a merchant even if a merchant already exists in your world. This can result in having only merchants in your world. The cause for this glitch is unknown.

History
