The Clown is a rare Hardmode enemy that appears during Blood Moons. The clown's primary weapons are smiley bombs which will do standard bomb damage to a player or NPC when they detonate (up to 100 damage), but will not destroy player-placed tiles (as of v1.2). Its bombs can be shot out of the air with most weapons. Touching the clown itself inflicts melee damage and has a chance to inflict the Confused debuff.

On the console/mobile ports of Terraria, or any PC version previous to v1.2, the smiley bombs it throws are treated the same as player-thrown bombs and will destroy nearby blocks when they go off, like a normal Bomb; this makes Clowns a serious hazard to any structures on these versions of Terraria.

Once you have defeated at least one Clown, the Clothier will start selling the clown costume.

Notes

  • Although Clowns are a relatively rare enemy, at least one Clown will usually appear over the course of any Blood Moon during Hardmode (More if the player is in the Jungle or has an enemy spawn increasing item in effect, like the Water Candle and/or Battle Potion).
  • Although the Bananarang rare drop is a weapon, due to its stackable nature (a feature shared only with Light Discs), the Clown may drop multiple Bananarangs at a time (up to four at least).

Console/ Mobile Tips

Console-Only Content
This information (or parts of it) applies only to the console version of Terraria.
  • Clowns are the only enemies that actually put structures in danger. Here are some tips to protect your work:
    • Replace your House's outer framework with Dungeon Bricks or raw Hardmode ores (Cobalt, Mythril, Adamantite), which are impervious to bombs.
    • Blocks that are impervious to explosions will not shield the blocks behind them though, so merely encasing your House with these will not provide much protection. A fair depth of a structure's outer framework will need to be replaced, or barricades made of impervious blocks can be constructed a fair distance away from vulnerable structures.
    • Build your house underground, or hide deep underground until the night is over, so that Clowns have no chance to spawn.
    • Travel several screens away from any structures you care to protect. Like all enemies, Clowns will only spawn near a character.
    • Build your house floating a fair distance above ground, or mine away enough blocks beneath and around your existing House (essentially making it floating), so that Clowns and their Bombs cannot reach it.
    • Put moats with Lava on either side of your base. Optionally you can install Active/Inactive stone drawbridges to make it easier for you to access your house. Note that Inactive Stone Blocks will still break from explosions.
    • With enough investment, a buffer of chests or storage items planted on tiles will offer complete protection against all explosives. See Storage Items.
    • The Clowns bomb have a 3x3 blast radius meaning a bomb proof wall must be 3 or more blocks thick.
