Mushrooms are a items that spawn randomly on above-ground grass. They are the weakest healing items in the game, restoring only 15 life each. Using them in this way is also inefficient as they can be crafted into Lesser Healing Potions that restore 50 life. Mushrooms are also used as ingredients in Regeneration Potions and Bowls of Soup. Players who use the potion hotkey may consume these by accident if they are in an inventory slot before the actual healing potion, so it is desirable to keep all preceding slots full or keep a permanent stack of mushrooms in a later slot.

Farming

Mushrooms grow on any free grass tile above ground, meaning they can be easily farmed by placing the grass in strips. The grass can be grown by connecting the dirt to existing grass, by planting Grass Seeds or using the Staff of Regrowth.

Only one tile of vertical clearance is required, so dirt strips can be placed close together in groups of three (more than that and you may not be able to harvest them), one tile apart vertically. Mushrooms will not grow if there is any type of wall behind them, so make sure the sky is visible behind the dirt strips.

Mushrooms do not require water. They can spawn while on-screen, unlike Trees.

With the Dart Trap mechanism, farms can be automated to some degree. The dart trap(s) can be linked to a Pressure Plate at the farm's entrance or a Timer. A launched dart will cut all grass and mushrooms that it comes into contact with, allowing the player to simply run across the farm and grab the dropped mushrooms without a single swing of their sword.

Trivia

The mushroom's texture is reminiscent of the mushrooms from the original Super Mario Bros.
