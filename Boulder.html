Boulders are unique placeable 2x2 foreground objects, and are stationary when placed. After placement, when mined with a pickaxe or drill, or when the blocks supporting them are mined, they fall and roll away, picking up speed until they collide with blocks; at which point they break and disappear. Boulders can not be picked up again once placed.

Boulders will pierce through unlimited enemies, players, and NPCs until colliding with solid blocks, causing extreme damage in the range of 60-150 per hit regardless of Armor and other defensive equipment. Boulders always roll left, unless there is no space for them to go left, in which case they roll right.

Boulder traps occur naturally in the Cavern layer, and consist of a single Boulder in the ceiling, supported by six (2x3) Active Stone Blocks Wired to Pressure Plates. If an enemy or player walks onto the Pressure Plate, the Boulder falls and damages any players or enemies it hits.

Notes

  • Defensive stats, including those provided by Armor, do not protect against Boulder damage. Boulders remove 60-150 Health points per hit regardless of your defense total (as well as those of enemies, NPCs, and other players).
  • As long as they have started rolling, Boulders cause the same amount of damage no matter how fast they are moving. A player or enemy hit by a Boulder that just began rolling and has not yet picked up momentum will take just as much damage as they would from a fast-moving Boulder.
  • Whenever open space is available on both its sides, Boulders always roll left. Blocks can be placed on their immediate left side to force them to roll right.
  • Boulders are not supported by Wood Platforms, Tables, and Workbenches. Placement on those will work, however the Boulder will fall through immediately, skipping its stationary state. If a Boulder has begun rolling and encounters one of those surfaces, it will fall through them.
  • Boulders can activate Pressure Plates.
  • Boulders can be placed on top of each other in a stack and will remain stationary. Mining the bottom Boulder causes all of them to fall and roll. Mining only the top Boulder causes only that one to roll off.
Anomaly: Selecting a Boulder in your Hotbar will cause all Wires to show on-screen, the way selecting Mechanisms does, even though the Boulder itself has no effect when wired.
Bug: NPCs will jump on top of the boulder like a platform, and hang off it as if they were floating.
Bug: When placing a chest on top of a Boulder and then making the boulder move will cause the game to crash.

Trivia

  • Boulders are the only placeable objects larger than one single tile in size that exist in the foreground (with the possible exception of the Door, which is in the foreground when closed). All others are either single foreground blocks, or background Furniture items.
  • Boulders are the only placeable objects that cannot be picked up again once placed, as Hammers have no effect on them, and mining them with a Pickaxe or Drill only causes them to roll.
  • At full tilt, boulders roll faster than a player with Well Fed buff, Werewolf buff, full Shadow Armor, a Quick Aglet, a Quick Anklet of the Wind, and 3 other Quick reforged accessories, or Quick Hermes/Spectre Boots. The boulder cannot be avoided by running in almost any circumstances, nor can a player ever catch up to a rolling one via running.
