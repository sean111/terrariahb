The Skeletron Hand is an item that allows the player to latch onto surfaces, similar to the Grappling Hook, Ivy Whip and Dual Hook. It is dropped by Skeletron.

Compared to the Ivy Whip:

  • The Ivy Whip can grapple up to three surfaces at a time, the Skeletron Hand up to two. Firing the third hook will automatically detach the first.
  • The Skeletron Hand has a shorter reach than the Ivy Whip by about 2 tile lengths.


Compared to the Dual Hook:

  • The Dual Hook can only grapple up to one surface at a time, but detaches when the second hook hits a wall, not when it is fired.
  • The Skeletron Hand has a shorter reach than the Dual Hook by about 7 tile lengths.
  • The Dual Hook emits a faint light, unlike the Skeletron hand.
  • The Dual Hook can be fired twice in quick succesion, which is useful if you're falling and missclick the first time.

Tips

  • Since the Skeletron Hand is available around the same time as the Ivy Whip, which is superior, it is recommended to try to craft the Ivy Whip instead of trying to get the Skeletron Hand.
  • When you use the Skeletron Hand with mouse-click rather than the hotkey, you can fire both grapples if the second is fired when the first is returning.
  • If you use the hotkey then a mouseclick to fire the Skeletron Hand there's no delay of firing the second grapple.
  • If timed right the Skeletron Hand can have three grapples out at one time, Using the hotkey, mouseclick, then mouseclick when the 2nd grapple is returning.
Bug : When having the Magma Stone, Fire Gauntlet, or Frost Armor equipped, or being under the effects of a flask imbuement, the Skeletron Hand does show the "fire" or "ice" effect as a melee weapon would, even though it is incapable of dealing damage. Other variations of the Grappling Hook do not exhibit this behavior.
Anomaly: The hand may sometimes pull the user through passages normally too small to fit through, if the player was able to grapple a block on the other side. This happens with all variations of the Grappling Hook as well.
Anomaly: The Skeletron Hand seems to let go of surfaces at ~1 minute, does not happen with Grappling Hook, Ivy Whip or the Dual Hook.
Anomaly: The Skeletron Hand seems to let go of vertical surfaces that are only one block thick and that have water opposite to the side that one is trying to grapple to. This happens with the Grappling Hook, the Ivy Whip and the Dual Hook as well. This happens because when you place a block in water the water no longer disappears and so the water is "inside" the wall and the hook conflicts with the block being both liquid and solid.
