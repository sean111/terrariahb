Arms Dealer

Arms Dealer
Statistics

The Arms Dealer is an NPC vendor that will spawn once the following criteria have been met:

  • There is an empty house.
  • You have at least one Gun or Bullet in your inventory (not just stored in a Chest).

The easiest way to acquire a qualifying item is to get the Musket by destroying a Shadow Orb; the first Orb smashed on any given world always yields a musket and some ammo.

If the Arms Dealer is killed, he will only respawn if the above conditions are met again. This means you must place a Gun or Bullet in your inventory. Having them stored in Chests will not fulfill this requirement, and the Space Gun and Laser Rifle don't count.

Items sold

Once the Arms Dealer has moved into a house, he will sell the following:

Inventory

Item Cost Availability
Musket Ball 7 Always available.
Unholy Arrow 40 Every night after defeating the Eater of Worlds for the first time (Always available after defeating the Wall of Flesh).
Flintlock Pistol 5 Always available.
Minishark 35 Always available.
Illegal Gun Parts 75 Every night.
Silver Bullet 15 During a Blood Moon (Always available after defeating the Wall of Flesh).
Shotgun 70 After defeating the Wall of Flesh.

Note: As with all NPCs, right-clicking an item will allow multiple purchases rather than purchasing one by one.

Names

The arms dealer may have any of the following names: DeShawn, DeAndre, Marquis, Darnell, Terrell, Malik, Trevon, Tyrone, Willie, Dominique, Demetrius, Reginald, Jamal, Maurice, Jalen, Darius, Xavier, Terrance, Andre, Dante, Brimst, Bronson, and Darryl.

Notes

  • While you have the trade window up, his stock will not change even when the requirements are met. Closing the window and bringing it back up fixes this.
  • The Space Gun and Laser Rifle do not count towards the 'acquiring a gun' requirement, and will not make the Arms Dealer spawn. This is because they use Mana as opposed to Bullets that the Arms Dealer would sell. The same is true for the Star Cannon, since he doesn't sell Fallen Stars, and the Blowpipe, as it is seed-based. He only arrives when you obtain Bullet-based weapons.

Trivia

  • The Arms Dealer is very arrogant compared to the other NPCs and seems to have an observant personality, often noticing you (apparently) intend to either buy the Minishark or steal one of his weapons. This, when coupled with the way he speaks, may support that he is based off the traditional gunslinger from old Western movies. He also seems to have a rivalry with the Demolitionist, as he says his products are better than "fireworks".
