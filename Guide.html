This page is about the NPC. For Terraria Online's Guide articles, see Terraria Wiki:Projects/Guides.

The Guide is the first NPC that you encounter. He provides tips that are mainly for new players to learn the basics of the game, and can show you all crafting recipes involving an item you show him.

Unlike other NPCs, the Guide spawns on World creation, before an appropriate House is available, but will move into the first one you build, and will teleport there if he can't get there otherwise. If he is killed, he will need proper housing before he respawns.

When the Guide Voodoo Doll is equipped, the player can attack the Guide.

If you wish to give your Guide a new name, or just feel like killing him, you can attack him while equipped with the Guide Voodoo Doll in an Accessory slot, and he will respawn if there is a house available and it is daytime.

Guide's tips

At the beginning of the game:

  • "You can use your pickaxe to dig through Dirt, and your axe to chop down trees. Just place your cursor over the tile and click!"
  • "If you want to survive, you will need to create weapons and shelter. Start by chopping down trees and gathering wood."
  • "Press ESC to access your crafting menu. When you have enough wood, create a workbench. This will allow you to create more complicated things, as long as you are standing close to it."
  • "You can build a shelter by placing wood or other blocks in the world. Don't forget to create and place walls."
  • "Once you have a wooden sword, you might try to gather some gel from the slimes. Combine wood and gel to make a torch!"
  • "To interact with backgrounds, use a hammer!"
  • "You should do some mining to find metal ore. You can craft very useful things with it."

As ore is inside your inventory:

  • "Now that you have some ore, you will need to turn it into a bar in order to make items with it. This requires a furnace!"
  • "You can create a furnace out of torches, wood, and stone. Make sure you are standing near a work bench."

As bars are inside your inventory:

  • "You will need an anvil to make most things out of metal bars."
  • "Anvils can be crafted out of iron, or purchased from a merchant."
  • "Underground are crystal hearts that can be used to increase your max life. You can smash them with a pickaxe."
  • "If you gather 5 fallen stars, they can be combined to create an item that will increase your magic capacity."
  • "Stars fall all over the world at night. They can be used for all sorts of useful things. If you see one, be sure to grab it because they disappear after sunrise."
  • "There are many different ways you can attract people to move in to our town. They will of course need a home to live in."
  • "In order for a room to be considered a home, it needs to have a door, chair, table, and a light source. Make sure the house has walls as well."
  • "Two people will not live in the same home. Also, if their home is destroyed, they will look for a new place to live."
  • "You can use the housing interface to assign and view housing. Open you inventory and click the house icon."

If you have unobtained NPCs:

  • "You should prove yourself by defeating a strong monster. That will get the attention of a dryad."

  • "Perhaps the old man by the dungeon would like to join us now that his curse has been lifted."

  • "I heard there was a powerfully wizard who lives in these parts. Make sure to keep an eye out for him next time you go underground."

Notes

  • The Guide will say "The ebonstone in the corruption can purified using some powder from a dryad, or it can be destroyed with explosives" even if the Corruption is not present
  • The Guide, like any other friendly NPC, will not respawn unless it is day time.
  • The Guide's crafting tips for weapons will show the adjusted damage with any bonuses for your currently equipped items, such as the Molten armor set bonus. Unequip damage adjusting items while speaking to the Guide to see base damage. This also applies to critical strike chance.
  • When the guide is dead the other NPC's know the name of the next Guide that will spawn, and will use it freely when talking about him.
  • The Guide must be killed to summon the Wall of Flesh, otherwise, the Wall of Flesh will not spawn.
  • The Guide is very important in the game, and pretty mysterious. He appears out of nowhere when the Player first starts, giving him/her tips and apparently knowing about everything that is happening on the Map (including things like the presence of a tied Wizard, or his knowledge on the Corruption and his "betrayal" by you activating Hardmode), and is the NPC that is represented on the Voodoo Doll. Finally, he must be killed in order to summon the Wall of Flesh and proceed into Hardmode
  • Additionally, the Cyborg refers to the Wall of Flesh as the "Wall of [Guide's Name]."
  • One of The Guide's names is Andrew, which is also the name of Andrew Spinks, the lead developer of Terraria. The Green Cap is dropped if you kill The Guide with that name, which is due to the fact that his sprite comprises of a player with the aforementioned cap.

Trivia

  • The Guide's name can be Maxwell, a reference to a character from Don't Starve and it also could be a reference to scribblenauts.


Bug 1.2.1.1: The Guide only suggests you to fight the Eye of Cthulhu when you have Fallen Stars in your inventory, while he should suggest it when you have Lenses/Suspicious Looking Eyes in your inventory.
Bug : The Guide sometimes will automatically make an item when you ask for a recipe. To prevent this, don't ask for a recipe when the Guide is standing near a crafting station. Instead of chairs and tables, consider using a bath and a toilet.
Bug : In worlds where the Crimson has been generated instead of Corruption, the Guide will provide messages to the player about the Corruption, such as requesting that you break Shadow Orbs, even if the player has broken Demon Hearts or even defeated the Brain of Cthulhu. Killing the Eater of Worlds seems to fix this.
Bug : One of the Guide's quotes is spelled incorrectly, leading to him mentioning a "powerfully wizard" rather than a "powerful wizard."
