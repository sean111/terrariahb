This information (or parts of it) applies only to the PC version of Terraria.

Chlorophyte Armor is considered to be a step up from the Hallowed Armor set, granting better defense and being geared more towards the DPS side for ranged and magic users.

Equipping the full set causes a leaf crystal to permanently appear over the player's head, attacking enemies for a moderate amount of damage, normally between 30-40 damage.

A full set of Chlorophyte Armor requires 54 Chlorophyte Bars. This converts to 324 Chlorophyte Ore.

Crafting the full set of Chlorophyte Armor PLUS every helmet requires a grand total of 468 Chlorophyte Ore.

This set is very useful for setting up AFK farms since many existing techniques were nerfed in 1.2.

Notes

  • The crystal will only attack one enemy at a time.
  • The crystal's damage does not appear to benefit from damage-increasing accessories.
  • When firing, the crystal launches a projectile moving in a straight line. As the projectile doesn't move too quickly, it will often miss if the target moves around a lot, such as a Wandering Eye.
  • In PvP, the crystal will NOT shoot at players, although its has been noticed that it will shoot right after the player hits anyone in PvP standing still.
  • When minions such as pygmies or the frozen hydra hits a mob with their projectile, the crystal will fire at the same mob (even if it has died) and the projectile will pass through walls. It only fires if the mob is hit with projectile. This also works with the player's projectiles, so one can easily dispose of enemies through walls using the Chlorophyte Partisan's spore clouds or other projectiles that go through walls.
  • The crystal can be very useful for farming certain mobs whilst inactive for their rare drops and endgame dungeon key molds. Encasing your character in blocks will render them invincible to the majority of attacks, whilst the crystal can fire through any blocks and kill enemies.
  • Although the crystal can, as stated, fire through blocks, it also appears to follow some sort of mechanic in which it 'prefers' targets in open line of sight. It will often refrain from firing at an enemy behind a wall, so do not count on it to assist you.
  • The crystal serves to alert the player of the direction from which an enemy is approaching in the dark since it will fire in that direction, in addition to slowing its approach due to knockback.
  • While floating above the player's head the crystal also emits a small amount of green light.
