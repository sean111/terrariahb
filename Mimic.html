The Mimic is an aggressive mob disguised as any kind of Chest. Mimics will chase the player when approached. They spawn after the defeat of Wall of Flesh or when a Chest Statue is activated. When spawned via the statue, no items are dropped.

Attacking Mimic

Mimics can take on the appearance of chests you would normally find in an area, such as normal brown chests closer to the surface, gold chests found underground, and even locked Shadow chests in The Underworld.

If a Mimic is hit by a ranged attack it will become active and start chasing the player. Mimics can sometimes be identified by the fact they do not always perfectly fit the 'grid' used, and thus do not line up with blocks as a normal chest would. A chest is most certainly a Mimic if part of it is above a hole, as regular chests can not be place over air.

Mimics are immune to Cursed Inferno and Confused debuffs.

Notes

  • A great way to farm these for their multiple rarer drops, is to use a Battle Potion and Water Candle during Blood Moon in hardmode. Players can easily get 7-10 to come to them with this method.
  • Using a Hunter Potion will make Mimics glow like any other enemy, identifying them.
  • Using a Spelunker Potion will not make mimics glow like a real chest.
  • The Mimics are slightly taller and thinner than a normal chest, and do not have a lock (except shadow chest variation).
  • Mimics disguised as Gold or Shadow chests will not sparkle, giving them away.
  • Mimics drop a large amount of money compared to most creatures.
  • An easy way to identify a mimic is if one appears in an area you frequently visit, such as a hellavator. If a random chest appears when you go off screen, it is almost definitely a mimic.
  • Three other easy way to identify mimics is to take all the chests and use them in your house. This way, you can always be sure that any chest you see is a mimic unless you are in an unexplored area. The second way is to simply shoot all chests that you find to see if they turn into mimics. The third way is to throw bombs at them; it will awaken them and deal heavy damage if it is a mimic; otherwise, the regular chest will not be harmed.

Trivia

  • Mimics first appeared in Dungeons & Dragons in 1977, and have appeared in countless RPGs since.
  • It seems that normal brown Mimics' only spawn requirement is a naturally generated dirt backwall; if you dig out a mountain but leave the backwall, Mimics can occasionally spawn.
Bug: Sometimes half of the Mimic can appear to be inside a block when the Mimic spawns next to a wall.
Anomaly: Sometimes spawns on top of other background objects (such as Life Crystals), giving them away, as real chests are background objects themselves.
Anomaly: They sometimes spawn on a diagonal slope, with only one block beneath them.
Anomaly: Sometimes, you can open a mimic somehow, however it will carry nothing. The Mimic will still attack, but it will be invisible.
