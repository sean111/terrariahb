Upon use, this item grants the player the Fairy buff and summons a glowing creature resembling a Pixie, which follows the character much like the Orb of Light. The color of the fairy is chosen at random from blue, green and red each time the bell is used.

If the fairy gets too far away from the player, it will teleport to their location. The fairy can pass through objects.

The summoned fairy is faster and brighter than the Orb of Light for the same mana cost, and so can be considered a direct upgrade. It is also possible to have both buffs simultaneously, in which case the character will be followed by two light sources.

Trivia

  • The fairy could be a reference to some games from the Legend of Zelda series, specifically Phantom Hourglass, where players had access to 3 different colours of Fairies, depending on what "Section" of the Triforce they were working with, Power, Wisdom, or Courage.
  • The colors of the fairies could be a reference to the three goddesses in Zelda: Nayru, Din, and Farore.
  • The Fairy Godmothers from the Disney animated film, "Sleeping Beauty" also glowed Blue, Green, and Red, although this could just be because they are three primary colors of light.
  • The name itself is probably a reference to Tinker Bell from Peter Pan.

Notes

Bug: If the player has a fairy out and saves and reloads a world the fairy may have a different color.
Bug: The Fairy Bell can be used when you have less maximum mana (0 or 20) than required (40).
Anomaly: The Orb of Light and the Fairy Bell can both be summoned at the same time.
Anomaly: The Fairy Bell, when used, always makes the same pitch, unlike the bell. However, the Magical Harp, which follows the same kind of creation, does play like the regular harp.
