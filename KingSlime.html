"The sky jiggles above you..."

King Slime is a boss that is, as its name would suggest, a very large slime: It is roughly 5 times larger than the Rainbow Slime (the largest ordinary slime). It is summoned with the Slime Crown, but can also spawn on its own rarely (but never in the center third of the map). As the King Slime takes damage, it spawns additional ordinary Blue Slimes. It will always drop one piece of Ninja clothes when defeated.

When King Slime spawns, a message appears in the bottom-left corner of the screen that reads: "King Slime has awoken!" Upon defeat, a similar message appears: "King Slime has been defeated!"

It is immune to the Poisoned debuff.

Random spawning

Aside from using the Slime Crown, King Slime will also spawn randomly based on the following conditions:

  • King Slime randomly spawns only in the two outer thirds of the map (the far left or far right). An easy way to determine if you are far enough is to check for the presence of bunnies, which spawn only in the inner third of the map, surrounding your original spawn point. If you can see bunnies, you are not far enough from your spawn point.
  • Goblin Scouts spawn in the same locations as the King Slime, so you'll know if you're in the right place if you encounter any.

Under these conditions, King Slime has a 1 in 300 chance to spawn. A Water Candle and/or the Battle Potion can speed up the process of encountering it.

Notes

  • While a large and resilient enemy and Boss music plays while fighting it, it is not a required step to advance the game. King Slime can be encountered regardless of the current level of advancement, and defeating it does not provide access to any critical items. It also does not travel through blocks, which creates shielding possibilities for the player that don't exist when battling other bosses. Also, it is easy to trap the King Slime and to get it with Melee Therefore, to some people it is not considered a boss.
  • Like other slimes, King Slime vanishes if the player travels far enough from the enemy (for instance, if teleporting away using the Magic Mirror, or if it is trapped and you walk away).
  • There can only be one King Slime spawned at a time in any given world. Attempting to spawn another using the Slime Crown will cause you to die instantly.
  • If King Slime is spawned in a Chasm, he has a chance of getting lost and stuck in one of the Chasms in The Corruption or Crimson.
  • The King Slime is a good source for Flamethrower ammo.
  • While the King Slime has the most useless drops (excepting slime, which is used as Flamethrower ammunition) and is a completely unnecessary boss to fight, its summoner is the most expensive pre-hardmode, and arguably during hardmode.

Strategy

Animation of King Slime
Killing King Slime
  • King Slime behaves exactly like a normal slime, except that he jumps much higher and further, and spawns other slimes after being hurt by the player. Due to its predictable nature, it is easy for a new player to defeat the boss with even lower tier weapons. The only thing a new player should worry about is dodging the King Slime as it jumps about, as he deals a relatively large amount of damage per hit. To finish the boss quickly, projectiles such as Shurikens, Spiky Balls, or Bows and Arrows are recommended.
  • Digging a pit two blocks wide and three blocks deep is an excellent tactic for fighting the King Slime, which is too large to fit in such a small pit. Placing wood platforms on top of the pit has the added benefit of keeping the smaller slimes out.
  • King Slime's absolute highest jump can clear a 17-block-high wall, so if you're planning on trapping him somewhere, make your walls at least 18 blocks high.
  • Fighting the King Slime with magic is relativity easy, due to the fact that the blue slimes it spawns drop stars, which refill 100 mana.
  • King Slime, unlike other slimes, does not skip along the water surface, but instead sinks. To avoid being swarmed by the slimes he spawns, consider luring him into a lake.
  • Advanced players can build an arena for farming Tattered Cloth,Slime Staff, and Ninja clothes. Build a base on where Goblin scouts are spotted, make sure that there are no pools of water on either side of that point and there are no caverns below, because goldfish, slimes and goblin scouts may spawn on those crevices. Have a lava moat on each side of your base, while making sure that the lava is low enough not to destroy any items dropping in it. Have Battle Potion consumed and equip a Water Candle to increase chances of King Slime and Goblin Scout to spawn.
  • A strategy using the Crimson Rod involves digging a pit low enough to conceal the player. Place a cloud above the pit and it will damage king slime as well as destroy any slimes that fall into the pit with the player. If done right the player will not have to do anything else.
  • A great way to take the King Slime out very easily is to find above or exactly his jump height, then stand upon a platform. Afterwards you can fire anything at the boss to take him out fairly quickly without taking any damage.

Trivia

The Ninja
  • Given the presence of the ninja inside, the king slime may be a reference to Ninja Gaiden, in which one of the first bosses is a slime-like creature that devours you.
  • The King Slime is the only slime that doesn't use the slime AI.
  • The King Slime is the only boss that does not drop a trophy.
  • The King slime is the only boss that drops a banner.
  • King Slime was also the name of a boss in the MMORPG Maplestory, which Terraria shares other elements with.
