The Grappling Hook is an item crafted from a Hook and three Iron Chains. It can also be bought from a Goblin Tinkerer for 2 gold. When used, the grappling hook will extend, latching onto anything it can reach; if it goes too far, it will retract. Once it latches onto a surface it will rapidly pull the player to it. The player will remain there until the player jumps, uses the grappling hook again, or 60 seconds have elapsed, after which the grappling hook will automatically detach itself.

The grappling hook provides convenience for navigating caves and chasms. When it attaches to a surface it will reset the counter on any distance the player may have fallen; as such it can be used to prevent fall damage. It can also allow players to reach ores on the undersides of large caverns. Additionally, it can be used as an anchor (shot directly into the ground below the player) when fighting enemies to prevent knockback.

Pressing the Grapple key (defaulted to E) will activate Grappling Hook. This allows the hook to be placed inside the player's inventory instead of using up a space on the hotbar.

Notes

Bug: The grappling hook can pull players through diagonal spaces that are normally too small for them to fit through.
Bug: Dropping the grappling hook (by using the throw key) while it is being used will not disengage the hook. However, this will not duplicate the grappling hook.
Anomaly: Opening a door while a player is grappled to it will fling the player forward.
Bug: Grappling any block/Wood Platform placed in a liquid (Only concerns Wood Platforms before 1.1) will cause the grappling hook to disengage immediately while launching the player in the direction of the tile
Anomaly: When falling a great distance, launching a hook at the ground will cancel fall damage.
Bug: If you grapple a Wood Platform and try to grapple another object, the Grappling Hook will grapple the platform again. The only possible way to grapple another object when this happens would be to jump off, then grapple your target object.
Anomaly: In Multiplayer, when warping sometimes you will still be grappled to what you were grappled to.
Anomaly: Grappling the ground and the jumping with good timing well launch you forward, similar to grappling a door. This can be used with the Hermes Boots to reach max speed almost instantly.
