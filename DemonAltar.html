Demon Altars are barnacle-like background items found Underground or in Chasms in The Corruption.

They are used to craft some boss summoning items and Night's Edge. You can tell if a Demon Altar is nearby by checking for a flickering light, though in a chasm a flickering light could also be from a Shadow Orb or Blinkroot.

If a player attempts to destroy the altar with any hammer other than the Pwnhammer or Hamdrax, or with them, but when Wall of Flesh is not defeated, the altar will be unaffected and the player will lose half their current health. If the Demon Altar is hit with the Pwnhammer or Hamdrax in the world where Wall of Flesh was defeated, the Demon Altar will be destroyed, blessing the world with one of the hardmode ores.

Smashing Demon Altars always results in Cobalt, then Mythril, then Adamantite. Smashing a fourth/seventh/etc. Demon Altar resets the sequence to Cobalt, and continues to bless the world with additional Hardmode mineral deposits. As most worlds have many Demon Altars to spare, this can be used to ensure that there is plenty of ore to go around on multiplayer worlds.It may be worth note that smashing Demon Altars is linked to the appearance of Wraiths. Take caution when destroying them in rapid succession.

Notes

  • You can destroy three demon alters and get all three ores.
  • Each additional altar destroyed makes the ores more common; at around twelve total destroyed altars the ores start to become about as common as Gold and Silver.
  • Destroying at least four altars is recommended before beginning the search for Cobalt.
  • Players about to enter hardmode may benefit by mining paths to several known demon altars beforehand, as navigating hardmode Corruption in molten armor is dangerous.
Bug: Sometimes demon altars will be generated encased in blocks, destroying the blocks covering it will destroy the altar.
Bug: If you die by hitting a Demon Altar repeatedly, an empty death message (just the character name) will be displayed.
Bug: If a demon altar is on top of sand, making the sand fall will destroy the altar. This, however, will not count as destroying it, and will not bless your world with the new minerals.
Anomaly: Demon Altars encountered outside of the corruption on a given map will always be found at the same depth (around 0 feet).
