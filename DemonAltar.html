Demon Altars are barnacle-like background items found Underground or in Chasms in The Corruption.

They are used to craft some boss summoning items and Night's Edge. You can tell if a Demon Altar is nearby by checking for a flickering, purple light, though in a chasm a flickering light could also be from a Shadow Orb or Blinkroot.

If the Demon Altar is hit with the Pwnhammer or Hamdrax in the world where the Wall of Flesh was defeated, the Demon Altar will be destroyed, blessing the world with one of the hardmode ores. If a player attempts to destroy the altar with any other hammer or in a world in which the Wall of Flesh is not yet defeated, the altar will be unaffected and the player will lose half their current health.

Smashing Demon Altars always results in Cobalt, then Mythril, then Adamantite ore being generated. Smashing a fourth/seventh/etc. Demon Altar resets the sequence to Cobalt, and continues to bless the world with additional Hardmode mineral deposits. As most worlds have many Demon Altars to spare, this can be used to ensure that there is plenty of ore to go around on multiplayer worlds, as ore is spawned even after there are all ores present in the world.

It may be worth noting that smashing Demon Altars causes the appearance of Wraiths. Take caution when destroying them in rapid succession.

Notes

  • Destroying at least four altars is recommended before beginning the search for Cobalt.
  • Demon altars are found at the same depth level all across the map, around 0 feet (where the dirt walls eventually stop). By digging a tunnel from the left to the right edges of the map at this depth, you will discover all of the demon altars generated.
  • Demon altars generate less and less new ores as more are destroyed. The second set of altars create 1/2 as much ore as the first set, the 3rd set creates 1/3 as much as the first, and so on.
  • Cobalt ore spawns below surface level, and Mythril spawns below rock level. Adamantite spawns below 1/3 of the distance from the rock layer to the bottom of the map.
  • Cobalt is roughly twice as common as Adamantite, with Mythril halfway between.
  • Each time a demon altar is destroyed, there is a chance a single random tile of rock in the world will be converted into ebonstone or pearlstone. This will more than likely grow to become a new underground Hallow or Corruption if given enough time.
  • Players about to enter hardmode may benefit by mining paths to several known Demon Altars beforehand, as navigating hardmode Corruption in molten armor is dangerous.
  • It's worth noting that if you destroy every Demon Altar in a world, it then becomes impossible to craft items that require one as their crafting station. Leaving one that's easily accessible in your world is a good idea.
Bug: Sometimes Demon Altars will be generated encased in blocks, destroying the blocks covering it will destroy the altar.
Bug: If you die by hitting a Demon Altar repeatedly, an empty death message (just the character name) will be displayed.
Bug: If a Demon Altar is on top of sand, making the sand fall will destroy the altar. This, however, will only bless your world with minerals in Hardmode.
Bug: Demon Altars encountered outside of the corruption on a given map will always be found at the same depth (around 0 feet).
